Residential

Sprinkler System Installation & Repair

  • We map each zone to real-world sun exposure, soil drainage, and plant types so you are not drowning one bed while another dries out. You review the layout before we dig, and nothing is “standard” unless it fits your property.
  • We install and service efficient rotors, sprays, and weather-aware controllers so coverage stays even as seasons change. Start with a simple clock or add smart scheduling when you are ready—either way the aim is less runoff and a lower water bill.
  • Lines are pressure-tested and leak-checked before we backfill, and you leave with a written as-built for future landscaping or repairs. If something is off at handoff, we correct it before we close the job.
  • Drip works when emitters, tubing, and pressure match your beds, pots, and mature roots. We document each run so water reaches the plants that need it—not the fence line—and you can expand a zone without redoing the whole layout.
  • Tubing stays tucked under mulch on routes we can reopen later for changes or repairs. You get a quick tutorial on what to watch for, and we are one call away if a line needs cleaning or rerouting.
  • Drip shares one timer program with your lawn zones when it makes sense, so you are not juggling two incompatible schedules. We align run times with local rules and show you how to tweak as the seasons shift.

Commercial

Commercial-level irrigation maintenance & management contracts

  • Visit cadence follows your property’s demand, irrigation windows, and what your contract covers—no mystery dates on the calendar. You know who to call, what each visit is for, and how we handle urgent valve or controller failures.
  • One record set covers valves, controllers, backflow assemblies, and warranty history so any technician steps in informed. When you renovate a zone or replace equipment, the map and notes are already on file—no retelling the whole story every time.
  • Contract sites get defined response expectations and scheduling priority when something can’t wait. You are not left guessing whether we will arrive before the weekend rush or the next heat wave.
  • Each round includes clock checks, coverage checks, and nozzle or pressure tweaks matched to the season and any local watering limits. Settings reflect your landscape’s needs—not a generic default—and we flag wear before it becomes a brown patch.
  • After startups and before peak heat we walk the system for wet spots, low pressure, and sticking valves. Small leaks cost less to fix early than redoing turf or explaining water damage to tenants or ownership.
  • You receive dated photos and plain-language notes after inspections—easy to forward to facilities or leadership. Recommendations spell out what is urgent, what can wait, and what to budget next quarter.

FAQ

Common questions

How long does installation take?

Most residential installs finish in one to three days depending on yard size, trenching, and controller upgrades.

Can you upgrade my old system?

Yes. We often retrofit smart controllers, high-efficiency nozzles, and drip zones without replacing everything at once.

Do you offer winterization?

We provide blow-outs and spring startups to protect pipes and keep coverage accurate after winter.

What areas do you serve?

We serve all of Orange County, California.
